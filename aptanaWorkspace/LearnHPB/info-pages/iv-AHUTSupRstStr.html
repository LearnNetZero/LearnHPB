SUPPLY AIR TEMP SET POINT RESET STRATEGY

Associated System: HVAC Distribution System

A supply air temperature reset strategy is a method of reducing the energy consumption of a central plant. Hydronic systems (for instance a chilled water loop) are designed to maintain a specific temperature differential under design conditions (specifically at the maximum cooling or heating conditions of a building). When a temperature set point reset strategy has been implemented, the supply air temperature set point will be adjusted depending on the anticipated heating or cooling demand. For instance, during periods of moderate to little cooling demand, the supply air temperature will begin to rise, possibly as a function of the outdoor air temperature or based on the current VAV damper positions within the air-side system. At a chiller, a higher supply temperature will result in reduced cooling demand as well as improved efficiency.

A supply air temperature reset strategy is a strategy that monitors the air-side system to determine if the supply air temperature set point can be raised or lowered. If the control system has determined that the cooling demand of the building has been met, a reset strategy will raise the supply air temperature set point according to a predetermined strategy (typically based on the outdoor ambient temperature).

Adjusting the setting will determine how the reset is implemented.

The value of this variable can be adjusted during a simulation run-time.
