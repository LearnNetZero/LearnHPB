MINIMUM OUTDOOR AIR INTAKE CONTROL STRATEGY

Associated System: Mixing Box

An outdoor air intake strategy will adjust the amount of outdoor air that is allowed into the mixing box. If a building has air-side economizers, the amount of outdoor air will be adjusted by increasing or decreasing the dampers in order to take advantage of "Free-cooling" which occurs when the outdoor air is at an optimum temperature and can supply the required cooling without introducing any mechanical cooling in to the system. during periods when the outdoor is outside of a desireable range, the outdoor air dampers when return to the minimum setting.

The value of this variable can be adjusted during a simulation run-time.
