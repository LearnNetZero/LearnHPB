OUTSIDE AIR RELATIVE HUMIDITY

Associated System: Weather data

This parameter sets the current relative humidty of the outdoor air. This value determines the amount of air conditioning required in order to maintain acceptable conditions within the building. Depending on the type of facility, the HVAC system may have to provide Humidification/ Dehumidification in order to stay within the established regional zones for indoor air quality. In addition, the parameter can determine the efficacy of HVAC systems that utililze Heat Exchanges between mediums for air and water systems

Adjusting this variable will cause changes in the response of the HVAC system. For instance, Raising the outdoor air dry-bulb from moderate conditions (around 65 Fahrenheit) to a warmer temperature (85 F) will cause an increased cooling demand and a reduction in the HVAC system's ability to take advantage of Air-side economization.

The value of this variable can be adjusted during a simulation run-time.
