MINIMUM OUTSIDE AIR DAMPER POSITION

Associated System: Mixing Box

This parameter sets the minimum amount of air required for proper ventilation of a building. Some buildings require a certain amount of air changes of outdoor air. The minimum air changes are determined by the type and location of a building and the expected number of occupants. If a building has air-side economizers, the amount of outdoor air within the building will be adjusted by increasing or decreasing the economizer's dampers in order to take advantage of "free-cooling". During periods when the outdoor is outside of a desirable range, the outdoor air dampers when return to their minimum setting.

Adjusting this value changes the minimum outdoor air setting for the building. It also affects the amount of heating and cooling required if the building uses air-side economizers.

The value of this variable can be adjusted during a simulation run-time.
