CHILLED WATER LOOP DIFFERENTIAL PRESSURE SET POINT RESET

Associated System: CENTRAL PLANT (CHILLER, COOLING TOWER, AND BOILER)

This parameter controls the loop differential pressure reset strategy. Hydronic systems (cooling/heating water loop) are designed to maintain a determined differential pressure under design conditions (maximum cooling or heating demand for a specific building). If the system utilizes two-way valves at the coils, these valves will modulate as required by the heating or cooling demands. As a result, the pressure across these coils (and hence across the entire system) will also modulate. In order to maintain a constant pressure differential, the water flowing through the system will adjusted by changing the motor speed of the water pumps. When a differential pressure set point reset strategy has been implemented, the pressure differential set point will be adjusted depending anticipated heating or cooling demand. For instance, during periods of moderate cooling/heating demand the pressure differential set point may be lowered, causing the average speed of the pump motors to be greatly reduced.

Adjusting the setting will determine how the reset is implemented.

The value of this variable can be adjusted during a simulation run-time.
